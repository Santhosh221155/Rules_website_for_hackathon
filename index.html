KYC and Financial Compliance Regulations

All financial institutions and fintech companies must comply with Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) guidelines as mandated by regulatory authorities.

  • Every customer must undergo identity verification using valid government-issued identification such as Aadhaar, PAN, or Passport.
  • Biometric verification (fingerprint or facial recognition) must be implemented for high-value transactions exceeding ₹50,000.
  • Companies must maintain transaction records for a minimum period of 5 years for auditing and regulatory review.
  • Suspicious transactions must be reported to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) within 24 hours.
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all user logins and financial transactions.
  • All financial data must be encrypted both at rest and in transit using industry-standard encryption protocols.
  • Failure to comply with KYC regulations may result in financial penalties, suspension of services, or legal action.

Data Privacy and Protection Regulations

All organizations handling user data must comply with global data protection standards to ensure privacy and user rights.

  • User consent must be obtained explicitly before collecting personal data.
  • Users must have the right to access, modify, or delete their personal data.
  • Data collection must be limited to only what is necessary for service delivery.
  • Organizations must implement data anonymization techniques where applicable.
  • Cross-border data transfers must comply with international data protection agreements.
  • Data breaches must be disclosed to users and authorities within 72 hours.
  • Non-compliance may result in fines up to 4% of annual global revenue.
